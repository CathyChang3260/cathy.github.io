Some Facts You Didn't Know About Garfield:

  1. Jon Arbuckle was mentioned as a cartooonist in the first comic published in 1978.
  2. Jon Arbuckle was originally going to be the main character, but the company Jim Davis worked for forced him to make it about the cat instead.
  3. Originally, Jon Arbuckle didn't own Odie. His friend owned him, but his friend lived with Jon.
  4. About 4 percent of the world's population reads Garfield on a daily basis.
  5. Garfield would be about 37 years old if he were a real cat. That is 18 years above his maximum lifespan.
  6. Garfield's favorite food is lasagna, but in Malaysia, there is a Garfield Pizza Cafe.
  7. Jim Davis's first comic strip was about a bug.
  8. Garfield was named after Jim Davis's grandfather.
  9. The Chicago Sun Post originally cancelled the strip, but readers protested, so they brought it back.
  10. Garfield wasn't Jim's only comic strip.
  11. There was going to be an amusement park built for Garfield, but it was scrapped later.
  12. Bill Murray agreed to do the Garfield movie because he thought it was written by one of the Coen Brothers, famous movie director.
  13. Davis's first job was as an assistant for the Tumbleweed's comic, another comic strip.
  14. Jim Davis once had 25 cats when he lived on a farm, hence the idea of a cat in his comic strip.
  15. Garfield had once been able to scare kids, like in the Halloween edition of the strip.
  16. Garfield takes place in Muncie, Indiana, where his grandfather lived.
  17. The characters in the strip evolved over time.
  18. Garfield has over 17 million Facebook fans.
  19. The comics were made for marketing and marketing only, not for entertainment.
  20. Garfield is a tabby cat with orange and black stripes.
